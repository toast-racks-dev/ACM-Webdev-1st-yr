Location & Geography

  • Located in the Mahalangur Himal sub-range of the Himalayas.
  • Formed by the collision of the Indian and Eurasian tectonic plates about 60 million years ago.
  • Situated on the border between Nepal and the Tibet Autonomous Region of China.
  • Coordinates: 27.9881° N latitude and 86.9250° E longitude.
  • Surrounded by peaks like Lhotse, Nuptse, and Makalu.

Exploration & Climbing History

  • First attempted in the early 1920s by British expeditions.
  • First successful ascent on May 29, 1953, by Sir Edmund Hillary (New Zealand) and Tenzing Norgay (Nepal).
  • Since then, thousands of climbers have reached the summit, with varying degrees of success and tragedy.
  • Climbing routes: South Col route (Nepal side) and North Ridge route (Tibet side).
  • Challenges: extreme altitude, unpredictable weather, avalanches, crevasses, and the "death zone" above 8,000 meters.
>

The Death Zone

  • The "death zone" refers to altitudes above 8,000 meters (26,247 feet) where the oxygen levels are insufficient to sustain human life for extended periods.
  • At these heights, the human body begins to deteriorate, leading to altitude sickness, hypoxia, and increased risk of frostbite and other health issues.
  • Climbers often use supplemental oxygen to mitigate the effects of low oxygen levels.
  • Many fatalities on Everest occur in the death zone due to exhaustion, exposure, and altitude-related illnesses.
